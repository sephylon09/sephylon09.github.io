1. Overview

LetterForge (com.sephylon.letterforge) is an Android word puzzle application developed and published by Mini Forge Dev. This privacy policy explains what information is stored on your device, what data third-party services may process, and what choices you have regarding your privacy.

LetterForge is designed to minimise data collection. Your gameplay data stays on your device. Where third-party services are used, they are disclosed here along with their purpose.

2. Offline gameplay and no account

LetterForge does not require an account, registration, or login of any kind. You can play all game modes, complete daily challenges, earn coins and XP, and track your progress without providing any personal information to Mini Forge Dev.

The core gameplay is available offline. LetterForge does not provide cloud sync. Your progress is stored locally on your device and is not backed up to a remote server operated by Mini Forge Dev.

3. Information stored locally

LetterForge may store the following types of data locally on your device. This information is not transmitted to Mini Forge Dev servers.

  • Game progress and current puzzle state
  • Daily challenge completion status
  • Coins and XP totals
  • Achievements and statistics
  • App settings and preferences
  • Game history

All locally stored data can be removed by clearing app data through Android Settings or by uninstalling LetterForge. See the Data deletion section for instructions.

4. Advertising and Google AdMob

LetterForge uses Google AdMob, a mobile advertising platform operated by Google LLC, to serve advertisements. Ad types include:

  • Rewarded ads — optional ads you may choose to watch in order to earn hints or other in-game assistance. The reward is granted only after the ad reward is earned.
  • Interstitial ads — full-screen ads that may appear at natural transition points during gameplay.

The presence of AdMob means the Google Mobile Ads SDK may process certain information in order to serve advertisements and measure their performance. This may include, but is not necessarily limited to:

  • Device identifiers and advertising ID (such as the Android Advertising ID)
  • IP address and approximate location derived from IP
  • App interaction and usage data
  • Diagnostics, crash, and performance information
  • Advertising and consent information

These advertising-related data types may be used by Google for:

  • Advertising and ad personalisation
  • Analytics and measurement
  • Fraud prevention and security
  • SDK performance monitoring

Data is transmitted securely where the AdMob SDK communicates with Google. Google's privacy policy governs how Google processes information through AdMob. You can review Google's privacy policy at policies.google.com/privacy and Google's advertising technologies at policies.google.com/technologies/ads.

Mini Forge Dev does not publish AdMob ad unit identifiers on this website. The publisher identifier used in app-ads.txt is disclosed separately for ad network authorisation purposes.

5. Consent and privacy choices

LetterForge may use Google's User Messaging Platform (UMP) to request advertising consent and present privacy choices where legally required, such as in regions covered by the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), and related regulations.

If consent is requested when you open the app, you will be presented with clear options before any personalised advertising is shown. You may be able to revisit your consent choices through the app's settings or privacy options menu where available.

You can also manage advertising preferences through your Android device settings under Privacy → Ads (exact path varies by Android version and device).

Where consent is not legally required, standard advertising practices may apply in accordance with Google's policies.

6. What the app does not access

LetterForge does not request and does not use the following:

  • Precise location
  • Contacts or address book
  • Camera
  • Microphone
  • SMS or call logs
  • Payment information

The app does not collect or transmit gameplay history to Mini Forge Dev or sell it to third parties.

7. Data sharing

Mini Forge Dev does not sell personal data. LetterForge does not operate its own backend server for saved gameplay data — all game data is stored locally on your device.

Third-party services such as Google AdMob may process data in accordance with their own policies. Mini Forge Dev does not control how Google processes data through the AdMob SDK.

8. Children and target audience

LetterForge is a general-audience word puzzle application. It is not specifically directed at children under the age of 13. Because the app uses Google AdMob, which may collect advertising-related data, it is not designed as a children's app under COPPA or similar regulations.

If you are a parent or guardian and believe that a child has provided personal information through the app, please contact us at the address in the Contact section below.

9. Data deletion

Because LetterForge stores all game data locally on your device, you can delete it at any time:

  • Clear app data: go to Android Settings → Apps → LetterForge → Storage → Clear data. This removes all locally stored game data including progress, daily challenge status, coins, XP, achievements, statistics, settings, and game history.
  • Uninstall the app: uninstalling LetterForge removes all locally stored data from your device.

For data held by third-party services such as Google AdMob, please refer to those services' own data deletion instructions or contact them directly.

10. Security

LetterForge stores your data locally on your device and relies on Android's built-in application sandboxing to protect it from access by other apps. Mini Forge Dev takes reasonable care in developing the application, but no method of electronic storage is completely secure.

Keeping your Android device updated and using appropriate device security features helps protect locally stored data.

11. Policy changes

This privacy policy may be updated when LetterForge adds or removes features, changes data-handling practices, or integrates new third-party SDKs. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page reflects the date of the most recent revision.

Significant changes will be noted in the LetterForge app update release notes where practical.
